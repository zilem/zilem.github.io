Have you ever grieved over the undue loss of your loved one so badly, your life's mission is dedicated to founding a social cause?


Sheepwolf

To save the person he loves most, he must be prepared to lose everything.

IN THE AFTERMATH of his brother's murder, a corporate climber turned social activist discovers his calling to save lives teaching the brutal art of self-defence, least expecting to save his daughter from her mom.

This feature screenplay is currently in development, to be produced in 2020.


S H A R E        

Synopsis

An epic journey of self-discovery and survival.

INGENU

LITTLE CHYE HUDDLES in a corner of his bed. Traumatized. He hears his older brother Moss whipped and punched by their father for hanging out with gangs. Just another day in the life of this low-income Singapore family during the 70s.

One day, little Chye returns home to find Moss taken away by police for heroin possession. Witnessing his father abuse his mother for negliglence, little Chye musters his courage. He stands up to his old man for the first time. In that moment, he learns to stand up for the weak.



IMMIGRANT

AT TWENTY, CHYE enrols in the University of Western Australia to study psychology in an attempt to understand himself. Working hard labour jobs to pay the bills, Aussies pick on him for no reason other than being Asian. Broken knuckles and a near loss of an eye prompts him to learn martial arts to defend himself.



CONFORMIST

AFTER GRADUATION, CHYE becomes fortuitously employed as a high level corporate executive in Singapore. For once in his life, he is able to amass wealth and finds love. Four years into his marriage with Jenny, at the brink of a divorce, they decide to salvage the marriage with a child. Nicole is born and becomes the centre of Chye's life. Jealous of her own daughter, Jenny abandons her at eight months and divorces Chye.



ACTIVIST

QUITTING HIS CORPORATE job, Chye becomes a full time Dad to shower Nicole with the love he never received as a child. Meanwhile, he puts his psychological training to use; counseling rape and sexual assault victims.

One day, he receives news that a street attack has left his older brother Moss in a coma. Grieving over Moss' plight, Chye discovers his calling. He travels around the world to learn true self-defence, returning to Singapore to found Asia’s largest realistic self-defence movement to protect and empower women.



FATHER

EIGHT YEARS GO by. Chye’s self-defense academy prospers. A new challenge arises. Chye receives a letter from his ex-wife’s lawyer petitioning for custody of Nicole. When the subordinate courts rule that Nicole is to be assigned to her mother, the heartbreak of being deprived of the one person he can’t live without drives him to take matters into his own hands...

Director's Statement

An overdue perspective on Masculinity & Racism.

BY THE END of my first meeting with Chye in Dec 2016, I knew this was someone I could work with, whose story I could see myself working on for the next 7 years. I completed my first draft in Jan 2018, which helped bring on board long time friend Arlene Bogna as co-writer. Through our collaboration, Arlene observed that…

Chye’s story was in fact my story.

His 1981 experience as an Asian in Perth was very similar to mine in New York 17 years later. Internalized Racism. Reverse Racism. Racial Microaggression. White Privilege. Chinese Privilege. Arlene and I would toil tirelessly over how to address these contemporary issues in our film.




IN THE TWO years that I shadowed Chye, I went through my own separation and divorce. I identified closely with his lifelong search for love, his disappointment and the grief following his divorce. From protecting his Mom to becoming a victim in his marriage.

The soft spot he had for women has become his blind spot.

The irony of Chye’s relationship with women manifests in his mission to protect and empower women while keeping his distance from them. He is fearful that he may succumb to his emotions and fall prey to yet another deleterious relationship.

This is a boy who got jaded before he had the chance to grow up.

It seemed to me that the breakdown and breaking free from a rules-based marriage was a timely catalyst that aided his departure from a discipline based martial arts to embracing the brutal & deceptive nature of true self-defence.




FOR DECADES, A toxic form of masculinity has prevailed in this world. This created a void of what men are supposed to be. How they are supposed to behave. Not until recently has there been a call for a new form of masculinity.

Masculinity based on vulnerability.

Some men may consider this weak. But vulnerability is not to be mistaken for weakness. Those who do may simply be insecure.

We hope our audience will find it liberating to see being Mr. Mom is not a loss of male pride. A man’s dignity is not determined by his wife's moral transgressions. A man’s judgment is not compromised when he appoints a young female to run his self-defence academy.

This is the new, non-judgmental masculinity our world deserves. It is the masculinity worth embracing and aspiring towards.
