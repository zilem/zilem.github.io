Why do Asians rush into university, study something they don’t enjoy, spend the next 30 years working at a job they don’t care for, with people they don’t like?

Gap Year Asian

Why escape Asia before Uni?

IF NEW YORK Times bestsellers, Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind by Yuval Noah Harari and The Subtle Art of Not Giving a F*ck: A Counterintuitive Approach to Living a Good Life by Mark Manson were to somehow have an Asian offspring, this book will be it.

It's a personal development book and a socio-anthropological critique of Asian vs Western values based on my experience living in the Anglosphere and continental Europe.

It is primarily written for Asian teenagers, who have just finished pre-University education. Also for the parents of these teenagers who can’t wait to arm their children with degrees so that they may fulfill the last leg of their financial responsibilities. To them, my advice is


Don't rush your life's journey.

It is a wake up call to compliant Asian populations to


Question the default.

Having said that, there would be some who would argue, “What makes you think I want to be awaken?” But these individuals do not return to their sleep blissful of their ignorance. Yes, there are those who do.

But for those who don’t, it's akin to living out some version of the locked-in syndrome, in which a person remains aware yet unable to move or communicate verbally due to a neurological disorder paralyzing the entire body. And so


They continue to feel helpless in their dreams.

This book is by no means a comprehensive scientific study. It is an anecdotal account of my personal experiences and observations of the individual operating within the collective.

For those who are interested, please stay in touch. This book is currently in development.
